What Trading Really Is

Trading is not gambling, luck, or fast money. At its core, trading is the structured exchange of risk for potential reward in a market driven by uncertainty. A trader accepts that outcomes are never guaranteed and instead focuses on managing probabilities across a large number of trades.

Professional traders approach trading like a business. There are losses, operational costs, emotional pressure, and long learning phases. The market rewards discipline, patience, and consistency rather than confidence or hope.

Why Price Moves in Markets

Price moves because of imbalance between buyers and sellers. When buying pressure exceeds selling pressure, price moves upward. When selling pressure dominates, price falls. Every candle reflects a battle of intent.

News does not move price directly. Markets move based on collective interpretation of information, expectations, and positioning. This is why price often reacts opposite to what beginners expect.
